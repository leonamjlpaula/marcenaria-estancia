# Design System: High-End Woodworking Editorial

## 1. Overview & Creative North Star
**Creative North Star: "The Master's Workshop"**
This design system rejects the "SaaS-ification" of digital interfaces. It is not a tool; it is a gallery. Inspired by the tactile precision of woodworking and the spatial pacing of *Kinfolk*, the experience must feel curated, quiet, and intentional. We achieve this through **Intentional Asymmetry** and **Tonal Depth**. By avoiding rigid center-alignment and embracing white space as a structural element, we mimic the layout of a premium art book. Every element should feel like it was placed by hand, not generated by a grid.

---

## 2. Colors & Atmospheric Tones
The palette is a dialogue between raw earth and refined craft. We move away from digital "pure" colors toward a "pigment-based" philosophy.

### Surface Hierarchy & Nesting
To achieve a premium feel, we prohibit 1px solid borders for sectioning (**The No-Line Rule**). Boundaries are defined by the transition of light and texture.
- **Surface Layering:** Use `surface-container-low` (#f7f3ec) for the main body of a page. When a content block requires focus, nest a `surface-container-lowest` (#ffffff) card inside it. This "paper-on-linen" stacking creates depth without clutter.
- **The Glass & Gradient Rule:** For navigation bars or floating image captions, use `surface` (#fdf9f2) with a 70% opacity and a `24px backdrop-blur`. This "frosted glass" effect allows the warm woodworking photography to bleed through the UI, softening the interface.
- **Signature Textures:** For high-conversion CTAs, use a subtle radial gradient from `primary` (#994422) to `primary_container` (#b85c38) to simulate the way light hits polished mahogany.

---

## 3. Typography: The Editorial Voice
Typography is our primary vehicle for sophistication. We pair a high-contrast serif with a neutral, invisible sans-serif.

| Level | Token | Font | Weight | Character |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Display** | `display-lg` | Newsreader | 400 | -0.04em tracking, 1.1 line-height. |
| **Headline** | `headline-md` | Newsreader | 500 | Tight letter-spacing. Like a masthead. |
| **Title** | `title-lg` | Inter | 500 | Medium weight for structural clarity. |
| **Body** | `body-lg` | Inter | 400 | 1.7 line-height. Generous "breathability." |
| **Label** | `label-md` | Inter | 600 | Uppercase with +0.1em tracking. |

**The Hierarchy Rule:** Display and Headline styles should be treated as "Art Objects." Do not center-align long blocks of serif text. Instead, use asymmetrical positioning (e.g., Headline left-aligned at 15% margin, Body right-aligned at 40% margin) to create a rhythmic, magazine-style flow.

---

## 4. Elevation & Depth
In this system, "Elevation" is a measure of light, not height.

- **The Layering Principle:** Avoid CSS shadows where possible. Instead, differentiate a "Product Detail" block from the "Background" by shifting from `surface` to `surface-container-high`.
- **Ambient Shadows:** If an element must float (like a "Contact Artisan" modal), use an "Atmospheric Shadow": `0px 20px 40px rgba(43, 31, 23, 0.06)`. The shadow color is a diluted Walnut Brown (#2B1F17), never grey.
- **The Ghost Border:** If a form field or button requires a container, use `outline-variant` (#dbc1b8) at 15% opacity. It should be barely perceptible, felt rather than seen.
- **Sharpness:** The `Roundedness Scale` is strictly **0px**. Every element—buttons, images, and containers—must have hard, architectural 90-degree corners to reflect the precision of a woodworker's chisel.

---

## 5. Components

### Buttons (The "Chiseled" Action)
- **Primary:** `primary` background, `on_primary` text. No border. 0px radius. Transitions to `primary_container` on hover.
- **Secondary:** Transparent background, `outline` border (Ghost Style, 20% opacity), `on_surface` text.
- **Tertiary:** `label-md` typography with a 1px `on_surface` underline offset by 4px. No container.

### Cards & Content Blocks
**Strict Rule:** No dividers.
- Separate "The Studio" from "The Collection" using 160px of vertical white space or a full-bleed shift to `secondary_container` (#d6eab2) for a "Sage" moment.
- Images should use `asymmetric aspect ratios` (e.g., 4:5 for portraits, 2:1 for workshop wide shots).

### Input Fields
- **Minimalist Frame:** Only a bottom border (1px, `outline-variant`). On focus, the border transitions to `primary` (#994422).
- **Labels:** Use `label-sm` placed above the line, always in `on_surface_variant` (#55433c).

### Signature Component: The "Provenance Tag"
A specialized `Chip` variant used for wood types (e.g., "Solid Walnut").
- **Style:** `surface-container-highest` background, `on_tertiary_fixed` text, `label-sm` font. No rounded corners.

---

## 6. Do's and Don'ts

### Do:
- **Do** use large, high-resolution photography where the wood grain is visible.
- **Do** utilize "Dead Space." If a section feels empty, it is likely perfect.
- **Do** overlap elements slightly (e.g., a serif headline overlapping the edge of a photograph) to create a layered, handmade feel.

### Don't:
- **Don't** use icons unless absolutely necessary. Use text labels.
- **Don't** use "Blue" for links or success states. Use `tertiary` or `secondary` tones.
- **Don't** use standard grid layouts. If a row has three images, make one larger than the others to break the visual "boringness" of the screen.
- **Don't** use animations that are "snappy." Motion must be slow, easing-in-out, resembling the deliberate movement of a craftsman.
